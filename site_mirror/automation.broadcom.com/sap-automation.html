    The system copy and refresh process is the single greatest waste of time and resources for SAP Basis teams. It forces your most valuable experts to do tedious manual labor – creating risk, reducing agility, and delaying critical business initiatives. With the 2027 retirement of SAP Landscape Manager (LaMa), now is the time to adopt a modern orchestration strategy with Automic, and eliminate this challenge forever.

    Explore Key Capabilities

    Orchestrate the Entire End-to-End Process

    Automic is a true "orchestrator of orchestrators," providing a single point of control to manage the entire lifecycle. It seamlessly automates everything from pre-copy preparations and infrastructure cloning to the final, complex post-copy application configuration steps—all in one unified and governed workflow.

    Eliminate Manual Post-Copy Errors

    In any refresh, the highest comes from the dozens of manual post-copy tasks that are required. Automic delivers certified, out-of-the-box automation for critical steps including BDLS execution, profile parameter changes, RFC destination updates, and user/role management. This ensures a consistent, reliable, and predictable outcome every time.

    Deliver with Confidence Using Predictive SLAs

    Move beyond reacting to failure alerts. Automic's intelligent SLA management monitors workflows in real time, analyzes historical performance, and provides advance alerts if a delivery is at risk of missing its deadline. This unique capability enables you to take proactive measures before a failure occurs, building trust between IT and the business. 

    “85% of surveyed IT professionals say they have had one or more SLA breaches in the last 12 months.”

    EMA, WLA Transformation Research, 2023

    Get Governed Self-Service for Your Business

    Transform your refresh process from a slow, ticket-based system into a modern, on-demand service. By leveraging Automic’s certified integration for ITSM platforms like ServiceNow, you can enable authorized development and project teams to request fresh SAP environments from a simple self-service catalog, while ensuring the Basis team retains full governance and control.
