    AI and Automation: The Engine of Digital Transformation

    The Foundation for an AI-Powered Future

    An AI strategy without the power of a modern enterprise automation engine is destined to remain an experiment

    In a landscape demanding unprecedented speed and intelligence, artificial intelligence (AI) and automation are no longer separate entities but powerful, interconnected forces driving unparalleled business agility.

    AI relies on vast amounts of data and structured processes – precisely what robust automation platforms provide. In return, AI injects unparalleled intelligence into automation workflows, enabling predictive insights, faster decision-making, and empowering more users. This creates a continuous feedback loop that is propelling enterprises towards a new era of innovation and efficiency.

    Automation Fuels AI: Building the Foundation for Intelligence

    Before AI can deliver its transformative power, it requires a reliable, consistent, and scalable foundation for AI data readiness. Automation provides this critical bedrock by ensuring data availability, workflow orchestration, and seamless connectivity that AI models depend on.

    60% of AI projects will fail to deliver on business SLAs and be abandoned in organizations without an AI-ready data practice1

    AI Powers Automation: Elevating Efficiency and Agility

    With a solid automation foundation in place, AI—especially Generative AI—transforms automation itself. This paradigm shift moves beyond simple task execution to enable smarter decision-making, extract actionable insights, and foster IT resilience and responsiveness.

    70% plan to implement AI-driven capabilities within the next 12 months and 91% identify AI-enhanced orchestration as extremely or very important.2

    The Strategic Imperative: Enterprise Orchestration for Agentic AI

    While agentic AI offers unprecedented capabilities, its full potential is unlocked only when managed within a robust enterprise orchestration framework. Without centralized oversight, organizations risk operational chaos, compliance breaches, and an inability to integrate AI into existing business processes.

    82% of enterprises plan to integrate AI agents in 1-3 years3
